About

Dr. Prince Gollapalli is a postdoctoral researcher in computational materials science at Technion - Israel Institute of Technology. He is currently working on atomistic-based approach of modeling materials for renewable energy technologies. He holds a B.Tech degree in Metallurgical and Materials Engineering from National Institute of Technology Warangal, M.E in Materials Engineering from Indian Institute of Science Bangalore, and Ph.D. in Metallurgical and Materials Engineering from Indian Institute of Technology Madras. He was a visiting scholar in the School of Materials Engineering at Purdue University.

For Ph.D., he worked on gaining atomic-level insights into anion migration from ceramic to metal by tapping into the predictive power of first-principles density functional theory based on the fundamental laws of quantum mechanics. As a visiting scholar at Purdue University, he was exploring into materials informatics to bridge the gap between physics-driven first-principles and data-driven machine learning approaches for better materials design.
